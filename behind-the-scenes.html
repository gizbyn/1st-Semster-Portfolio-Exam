Behind About

... behind the scenes or building this website.

How did it start?


It was a beautiful May night. I was in my bed. Sleeping. Dreaming. Suddenly, I woke up. I sat up and said: "I know what my portfolio website is going to look like."
No, of course not. I am just joking. To be honest, I precisely do not know when or how it started. But I know for sure, that I wanted to add some background pictures of myself the the website. So I asked my good friend Aivars to take some photos of me. He agreed, so we went to Christiania and did it. I am thankful for his time and editing and photography skills. Oh, by the way the yellow hoodie I didn't choose randomly. I wanted a yellow color to be a part of my portfolio.

And what was next?


Well, I had some ideas about the look of my website. But were they good? Were they solid? Were they worth my time? There were no other options than draw it down. Because the website has to be responsive, I needed to create multiple wireframes for multiple devices. So finally, I made the wireframes and I have attached two of them (one for a desktop and the second for the mobile) next to this box. I am sorry for you not seeing a masterpiece, but since they are sketches, I do not draw them half-an-hour (each), but I just put ideas on the paper. You can also see my notes as a part of the desktop wireframe.



The link to the desktop wireframe.

The link to the mobile wireframe.

Wait, wait, wait... what about the design?


As I mentioned before, the choice of yellow color was not by accident. Okay. And what about other colors? I wanted to keep it minimalistic, but at the same time, I wanted to express myself and my personality. So another two colors were definitely black and white (colors which do not change my idea of a minimalistic design). I did stick with these three colors but one teacher advised me to use desaturated yellowish color to add boxes around the text to adjust readability (because I had a different composition than I do have now). That is how my box-layout scheme was created! However, I needed to put everything together to see what my design will look like. So I created a moodboard and a style tile which can be seen beneath (note: they are clickable).

That's it? And the code?


Of course, I coded. After design issues, I moved on to the actual coding. I started with the main page, the menu, background image etc. Something worked, something did not. I used (for the first time) flexbox, which was something completely new for me and I am very grateful for doing so. I appreciate learning some new skills. Even though I had an idea of the design, it was changing, evolving and I was trying out new things. Well, this is the process of development, what else could I have expected? I also included some animations (I hope, that you have discovered some of them - although I am sorry for you - using the mobiles because the animations were created mostly for the desktop user).

You can have a look at some of my codes from the website by clicking on this link and this link.

Was it easy?


I can tell you - it wasn't. But it was challenging and interesting. The hardest part for me was to start! After starting to work on it, I was falling asleep in my bed thinking about this project, what I am going to do tomorrow and what I can include (and I am serious). And no, I am not a geek. Maybe a little bit a weirdo.
Anyway, if I did not know anything, I searched it on the internet or asked for help (thank you all). I also asked other people about their opinions and received some feedback.

Even though it was not easy, I really enjoyed the process of making my portfolio. I hope that the result has a value not only to me but also to you.
